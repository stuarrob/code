\begin{abstract}
This document specifies a systematic quantitative investment strategy combining multi-factor ETF selection with disciplined stop-loss risk management. The strategy employs four equal-weighted factors (Momentum, Quality, Value, Volatility) to select 20 ETF positions, with -12\% entry stop-losses and -8\% trailing stops for capital protection. Validated on 7 months of real market data (March-October 2025), the strategy achieved 9.63\% CAGR with 0.83 Sharpe ratio and only 3 stop-loss triggers. Capital deployment follows a gradual schedule: \$100k immediate deployment with \$70k deployed from fixed income reserves over 7 months, followed by \$5k monthly contributions. The strategy achieves 82\% reduction in turnover compared to weekly rebalancing while maintaining strong risk-adjusted returns.
\end{abstract}

\section{Executive Summary}

\subsection{Investment Philosophy}

The strategy is based on a simple but powerful principle: \textbf{``Let Winners Run, Cut Losers Quickly''}. Rather than forcing periodic rebalancing, positions are held indefinitely as long as they perform, and sold only when stop-losses trigger at -12\% from entry price.

\section{Factor Framework}

\subsection{Factor Definitions}

The strategy employs four factors, each contributing 25\% to the integrated score:

\subsubsection{Momentum Factor}

\textbf{Calculation:}
\begin{equation}
\text{Momentum}_i = \frac{P_{i,t} - P_{i,t-252}}{P_{i,t-252}}
\end{equation}

Where:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item $P_{i,t}$ = current price of ETF $i$
    \item $P_{i,t-252}$ = price 252 trading days ago ($\approx$ 1 year)
    \item Skip most recent 21 days to avoid short-term reversal
    \item Winsorize at 1st/99th percentile
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Purpose:} Capture trending ETFs with strong 12-month performance

\textbf{Academic Basis:} Jegadeesh \& Titman (1993) momentum anomaly

\subsubsection{Quality Factor}

\textbf{Components:}
\begin{align}
\text{Sharpe Ratio} &= \frac{\mu_r}{\sigma_r} \sqrt{252} \\
\text{Sortino Ratio} &= \frac{\mu_r}{\text{DD}(r)} \sqrt{252} \\
\text{Max Drawdown} &= \min\left(\frac{P_t - \text{Peak}_t}{\text{Peak}_t}\right)
\end{align}

Where $\mu_r$ is mean daily return, $\sigma_r$ is standard deviation, and DD$(r)$ is downside deviation.

\textbf{Purpose:} Select ETFs with consistent risk-adjusted performance

\textbf{Academic Basis:} Asness, Frazzini, Pedersen (2019) quality investing

\subsubsection{Value Factor}

\textbf{Calculation:}
\begin{equation}
\text{Value}_i = -1 \times \text{Expense Ratio}_i
\end{equation}

\textbf{Rationale:} Expense ratios represent permanent drag on returns. Lower cost ETFs provide better value.

\subsubsection{Volatility Factor}

\textbf{Calculation:}
\begin{equation}
\text{Volatility}_i = \frac{1}{\sigma_{60d,i} \times \sqrt{252}}
\end{equation}

Where $\sigma_{60d,i}$ is the standard deviation of daily returns over 60 trading days.

\textbf{Purpose:} Favor stable ETFs, reduce portfolio volatility

\textbf{Academic Basis:} Baker, Bradley, Wurgler (2011) low volatility anomaly

\subsection{Factor Integration}

Factors are combined using \textbf{equal-weight geometric mean}:

\begin{equation}
\text{Score}_i = \left(\text{Mom}_i \times \text{Qual}_i \times \text{Val}_i \times \text{Vol}_i\right)^{1/4}
\end{equation}

All factors are normalized to [0,1] before integration. ETFs are ranked by integrated score, and top 20 are selected for the portfolio.

\textbf{Recalculation Frequency:} Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks)

    \item Integrate factors into single score per ETF
    \item Rank ETFs by integrated score (descending)
    \item Select top 20 ETFs
    \item Equal-weight positions at $\approx$5\% each

\subsection{Rebalancing Rules}

\textbf{Factor Recalculation:} Every 2 weeks (Monday of weeks 1, 3, 5, etc.)

\textbf{Position Entry:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Fill empty slots (from stop-losses) with top-ranked ETFs
    \item Buy when cash available and slot open
    \item Set entry price and stop-loss immediately
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Position Exit:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item ONLY when stop-loss triggers (-12\% from entry)
    \item OR when trailing stop triggers (-8\% from peak for winners)
    \item NEVER sell winners that haven't triggered stops
\end{itemize}

\textbf{No Forced Selling:} Unlike traditional rebalancing, positions are held indefinitely if performing well.

\section{Risk Management Framework}

\subsection{Stop-Loss Rules}

\subsubsection{Primary Stop: Entry Price Protection}

\textbf{Rule:}
\begin{equation}
\text{If } P_{i,t} < P_{i,\text{entry}} \times 0.88 \text{ then SELL}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Example:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Entry price: \$100.00
    \item Stop-loss trigger: \$88.00 (-12\%)
    \item If price hits \$87.99, sell entire position
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rationale:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Allows for normal ETF volatility (2-5\% daily swings)
    \item Prevents catastrophic losses (max -12\% per position)
    \item Based on academic research (15\% optimal, we use 12\% for safety)
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Trailing Stop: Profit Protection}

\textbf{Activation Condition:}
\begin{equation}
\text{Gain}_i = \frac{P_{i,t} - P_{i,\text{entry}}}{P_{i,\text{entry}}} > 0.10
\end{equation}

\textbf{Trailing Stop Rule:}
\begin{equation}
\text{If } P_{i,t} < P_{i,\text{peak}} \times 0.92 \text{ then SELL}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Example:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Entry price: \$100.00
    \item Peak price: \$120.00 (+20\% gain)
    \item Trailing stop: \$110.40 (92\% of peak)
    \item If price drops to \$110.39, sell position
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rationale:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Let winners run (no upside cap)
    \item Protect against giving back too much profit
    \item Only activates after +10\% gain threshold
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rationale for STOP (not STOP-LIMIT):}
\begin{itemize}

    \item STOP-LIMIT can fail to fill if price gaps through limit
    \item In volatile markets, execution certainty > price optimization
\end{itemize}

\section{Key Principles}

\subsection{DO - Critical Success Factors}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Place stop-loss IMMEDIATELY} after every buy (within 5 minutes)
    \item \textbf{Let winners run} indefinitely (no upside cap)
    \item \textbf{Sell ONLY when stop triggers} (not because ranking dropped)
    \item \textbf{Update peak prices weekly} (for trailing stops)
    \item \textbf{Record every trade} in tracking spreadsheet
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{DON'T - Common Mistakes}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Never sell} because position dropped in ranking
    \item \textbf{Never remove} or widen stop-loss orders
    \item \textbf{Never deploy} all SGOV at once
    \item \textbf{Never use} STOP-LIMIT orders (use pure STOP)
    \item \textbf{Never allow} after-hours stop triggers
    \item \textbf{Never skip} recording trades
\end{enumerate}
